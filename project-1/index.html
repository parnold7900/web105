My life's narrative weaves through the disciplined paths of the United States Army to the serene embrace of nature and the energizing atmosphere of the gym. My military journey began in 2008, immersing me in the rigorous demands of basic training in Kentucky and specialized training at the Aberdeen Proving Ground in Maryland. As a wheeled vehicle mechanic, and later as a Stryker Systems Maintainer and Stryker Recovery System Operator, I acquired skills that not only served the Army but also instilled in me a profound sense of responsibility and excellence. Rising to the rank of sergeant, I led with pride, serving in Hawaii and contributing to operations in Iraq, before concluding my service as the operations NCOIC at Joint Base Lewis McCord in 2015. Parallel to my military career, my passion for fitness and working out has been a constant source of strength and renewal. This passion, reignited after my service, became a daily refuge and a form of meditation, allowing me to maintain both physical and mental health. My commitment to fitness sees me in the gym 4 to 6 times a week, a habit I cherish not only for the benefits it brings to my life but also as a continuation of the discipline I embraced in the Army. Moreover, my life is richly colored by my love for fishing, a tranquil activity that bonds me with my young son and connects us to the beauty of Lake and McHenry counties and the cherished memories of Cable, Wisconsin. These fishing trips are not just about the catch; they're about imparting lessons of patience and perseverance, and savoring the quiet moments that life offers. In sum, my journey is one of continuous growth, shaped by the values learned in the Army, the discipline of regular workouts, and the peace found in fishing. Each aspect of my life complements the others, creating a balanced existence that I am eager to share and live to its fullest.
